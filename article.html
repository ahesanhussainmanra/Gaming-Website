Why Most Players Miss Easy Duels

Players usually aim only when they see an enemy. The fix is pre-activation: hold likely head-level angles before visual contact. This shrinks reaction dependency and improves first-bullet success.

Phase 1: Route Mapping

Before round start, mark two danger lanes and one fallback position. Move with crosshair aligned to first threat lane. Shift only after lane is cleared.

Phase 2: Height Anchoring

  • Use static objects like door handles or wall lines as head-height references.
  • Avoid crosshair drift while sprinting between cover blocks.
  • Reset height instantly after jump or utility usage.

Phase 3: Entry Timing

Slice corners with micro stops. Let movement settle before firing. This reduces panic spray and keeps your opening duel controlled.

Weekly Progress Check

Track opening kill ratio and first-shot accuracy at the end of each week. If both metrics move up, your discipline system is working.
